Homebrew For Mansions of Madness: 2nd Edition
Play
 

Rules

Select expansions to add additional rules. (Physical copy of board game required).

Players are divided into teams with the aim of collecting all the relics and summoning Cthulhu or eliminating all players on the opposing team/s.

The game is played over a series of rounds. Each round consists of two phases that are resolved in the following order:

  1. Player phase:
    • Players take turns to perform actions. (see Player phase, turn and actions)
    • Each team alternates player turns/actions until all players have taken their turn/actions.
  2. Mythos phase:
    • After each player has had their turn, a Mythos phase starts. (see Mythos Phase)
    • All players must resolve the required checks (if they affect them) during this phase.

After each Mythos phase, the players start a new round beginning with the Player phase. Players continue resolving game rounds until they have won or lost the game.

The game ends when either:

  • One team has all 4 relics in their possession and successfully summons Cthulhu.
  • Or have eliminated the players on the opposing team/s.

Individual players that have gone insane and are not eliminated may also win if they complete their Insane conditions.

This custom game mode requires a 6-sided die required for the Move action

Setting up the board

  • Configure your own board using Mansions of Madness tiles. (Ideally in 6x4 or 6x5 grid)
  • Or use the Mansions of Madness Board generator app to generate a tile configuration for you.
  • Randomly place 12 Search tokens in different spaces on the board.
  • Fix up any doors or walls on the board using the door and wall tokens (if required).
  • Place an Explore token on a random door - this will act as a locked door.

Setup player teams

  • It is recommend to split players into either:
    • For 4 players: 2 teams of 2
    • For 5 players: 2 teams of 2 (with the spare person acting as a Dungeon Master)
    • For 6 players: 2 teams of 3 or 3 teams of 2

Distribute starting items

  • Use the app to generate and distribute items for each team member.
  • If your investigator has an ability that gives them a starting item, collect that item.
  • Players keep and can re-use all items and spells collected unless a condition requires a player to drop an item or spell (relics are retained unless player is eliminated), in which case they drop it in the space they are in. Dropped items can be collected by other players as an action.

Place your investigators on the board

  • Each player may place their investigator on the board anywhere around the perimeter (or agreed upon by all players).
  • This is where you'll start the game.

Additional Info

  • When resolving tests:
    • Players may use Clue tokens to convert   to   during any check.
  • A player is eliminated if they accumulate Damage exceeding their total Damage.
    • When they are eliminated, they drop all collected relic items in the space they died in.
    • When a player is eliminated, they come back as a guardian monster. (Refer to Guardian monster and actions below).
  • When a player accumulates Horror exceeding their total Horror, they become Insane and must follow the conditions on their card.
    • Individuals may also win if they complete their Insane conditions.

Relics are Unique Items that are required in order for a team to win. There are 4 relics that are hidden in the Search tokens. Once a team collects all 4 relics and summons Cthulhu, they win the game.

If a player is eliminated (accumulates their Damage exceeding their total Damage) they drop any relics they have collected in the space they died.

  • Relics will be 4 Grotesque Stone Unique Item cards.
  • Relics will be the 4 Relic Unique Item cards from the Path of the Serpent ( ) expansion.
  • Relics will be 4 Unique Item cards from the Streets of Arkham ( ) expansion. These items have negative conditions when holding them:
    • Mysterious Idol: When you suffer 1 or more Damage, become Dazed.
    • Star Calendar: If you move more than a single space as part of a move action, suffer 1 facedown Damage.
    • Stone Chest: When you perform a move action, you can only move up to 1 space.
    • Strange Tablet: When you suffer 1 or more Horror become Dazed.

Each player may perform 1 Move action and 2 additional actions from the following:

  • Move: Move X (die roll) spaces.
    • If a player enters/moves through a space with a monster, they suffer 1 facedown Horror.
    • Refer to Place a trap for when a player enters/moves through a space with a trap.
  • Search: Search tokens for relics, items, spells, or keys. (Check required)
  • Attack: Attack an opposing player or summoned monster. (Check required)
    • Attacking can only be done if the opposing player is adjacent to or within 1 space of the attacking player unless attacking with a firearm or spell (within range of 2 spaces).
    • You cannot attack another player behind a door or wall.
    • If a player is eliminated, they must drop any relic items they have collected in the space they died. Other players may collect these items as an action.
  • Heal: Heal yourself or another ally. (Check required)
    • A player may discard Damage or Horror equal to the number of  .
    • Or remove a negative condition (except those caused by holding relics).
  • Trade: Trade an item or relics with an ally. Multiple relics maybe traded.
  • Steal: Steal an item/relic from an opposing player. (Check required)
    • A player may only steal 1 item or relic from an opposing player per steal action. I.e. you cannot steal all relics from a player in one steal action.
  • Place a trap: A player may place a trap to inhibit other players or defend themselves from particular actions (e.g. attack, steal, etc.). Traps affect everyone (except the player placing them on their turn) and may be part of a player's Insane condition. Traps do not affect the player placing them on their turn but take effect after their turn. I.e. if a player places a trap in their turn they are unaffected but will be affected if they move through or are in a space with a hazard, on their next turn.

  • Disarm a trap: Disarm a trap. (Check required)
    • A player may disarm a trap if they are in or adjacent to a space containing a trap.
    • Players cannot disarm traps behind doors or walls.
    • A player with the Fire Extinguisher item may disarm a fire trap in or adjacent to their space as a free disarm action at the start of their turn.
  • Unlock door (free action): Unlock a door (Explore token). (Key required)
    • Players are able to find a key (through the Search action) to use to unlock a locked door.

  • Collect dropped item: Collect a dropped item that is adjacent to or in the same space as a player.
  • Collect dropped relic: Collect a dropped relic (from an eliminated player) that is adjacent to or in the same space as a player.

  • Move, Move, Move
  • Move, Attack, Attack
  • Move, Steal, Move
  • Heal, Steal, Move
  • Steal, Place a trap, Move
  • etc.

After everyone has taken their turn/actions, the Mythos phase begins, after which it cycles back to the Player phase and repeats.

After all players have taken their turn/actions, the Mythos phase begins. During this phase, monsters either spawn on the board or move and attack. Players must resolve the required checks.

Once all players have resolved their checks, it cycles back to the Player phase where players can take their turns again and the cycle repeats.

Monster damage affects all players regardless.

Choose from one the options for spawning and controlling monsters during the Mythos phase to be used for the entire game:

Team controlled Monsters

Monsters in the Mythos phase are controlled by each team. Monster damage affects all players regardless.

  • First Mythos phase:
    • Each team selects a monster and places it anywhere on the board.
    • Click the Start Mythos button in the app to generate Mythos effects and player checks.
    • Players resolve any checks.
    • Players phase restarts.
  • Subsequent Mythos phases:
    • Each team moves their chosen monster (per agreed upon monster movement e.g. 1 or 2 spaces, random position, etc.).
    • Click the Start Mythos button in the app to generate Mythos effects and player checks.
    • Players resolve any checks.
    • Players phase restarts.
  • If a monster is killed: Spawn another monster to take it's place.

Dungeon Master controlled Monsters

A designated person (preferably not participating in either team) can choose the monsters to spawn (during first Mythos phase) and move them at their discretion each subsequent Mythos phase. If a monster is killed, they may choose another monster to spawn/control next Mythos phase. Monster damage affects all players regardless.

  • First Mythos phase:
    • Dungeon Master selects monster/s and places them anywhere on the board.
    • Click the Start Mythos button in the app to generate Mythos effects and player checks.
    • Players resolve any checks.
    • Players phase restarts.
  • Subsequent Mythos phases:
    • Dungeon Master moves each monster.
    • Click the Start Mythos button in the app to generate Mythos effects and player checks.
    • Players resolve any checks.
    • Players phase restarts.
  • If a monster is killed: Spawn another monster to take it's place.

App controlled Monsters

Use the app to randomly generate and move monsters.

Once a team has collected all four relics, the player to acquire the last relic (regardless of whether they acquired it in their last action of their turn) must summon Cthulhu. Only when they have successfully passed their summon Cthulhu check does the team win the game.

If they fail their summon check, the game resumes from the current players's turn/action. Any team member may perform the summon Cthulhu action as part of their turn/actions.

If a player has been eliminated, they can spawn on the board as a Guardian monster. During their turn they may perform 3 actions from the following:

  • Move: Move X (die roll) spaces.
  • Attack: (see above)
  • Place a trap: (see above)

Guardian monsters cannot use any items, nor collect relics.
